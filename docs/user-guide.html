Digital Signing

Prove authenticity and verify integrity of any document, photo, or text using cryptographic signatures tied to your Dogecoin address.

What is Digital Signing?

Digital Signing uses your Dogecoin private key to create a cryptographic signature that proves:

  • Authenticity: You created or approved the content
  • Integrity: The content hasn't been modified or tampered with
  • Non-repudiation: You cannot deny having signed the content
  • Timestamp: The content existed at the time of signing

How It Works

Technical Process:
  1. Content is hashed using SHA-256 to create a unique fingerprint
  2. Your private key signs the hash using ECDSA cryptography (secp256k1 curve)
  3. The signature is encoded in Base64 format
  4. Your Dogecoin address is included for verification
  5. Anyone can verify the signature without exposing your private key

What Can You Sign?

Text Documents: Sign emails, contracts, agreements, or any text content
Files: Sign PDFs, images, videos, or any file type by generating its SHA-256 hash
Photos: Take a photo with the camera and sign it instantly to prove authenticity and timestamp

Use Cases

  • Legal Documents: Sign contracts, agreements, or legal notices with cryptographically provable authorship
  • Proof of Authenticity: Prove you took a specific photo or created content at a certain time
  • Business Records: Sign invoices, receipts, or transaction records
  • Content Verification: Prove content hasn't been altered since signing
  • Identity Verification: Digitally sign documents to prove your identity
  • Licensing: Sign software, artwork, or creative work with your cryptographic signature

Digital Signatures vs NFTs / DRC-20 / Doginals / Ordinals

Comparing digital signatures with blockchain-based attestations:

Digital Signatures - Advantages:
  • No On-Chain Storage: Documents/photos aren't stored on blockchain, saving space and fees
  • Instant Verification: Verify signatures offline or online without waiting for blockchain confirmations
  • Low Cost: Free to sign, no transaction fees required
  • Privacy: Content remains private, only the hash and signature are shared
  • Offline Capable: Sign and verify without internet connection
  • Lightweight: Signatures are small (64-70 bytes), easy to share
  • Proven Technology: Based on established cryptographic standards (ECDSA, SHA-256)
  • No Network Congestion: No dependency on network state or gas fees
  • Reversible: Signatures don't permanently consume blockchain resources
  • Unlimited: Sign unlimited documents without blockchain constraints
NFTs / DRC-20 / Doginals / Ordinals - Limitations:
  • On-Chain Storage Required: Data must be stored on blockchain, consuming network resources
  • High Fees: Pay transaction fees for every attestation
  • Network Dependency: Requires blockchain consensus and confirmations
  • Storage Limits: Block size limitations restrict content size
  • Privacy Concerns: Content is permanently on public blockchain
  • Scalability Issues: Every signature permanently adds data to blockchain
  • Environmental Impact: Requires blockchain consensus validation
  • Network Congestion: Can fail during high network usage
  • Permanent: Cannot be removed once added to blockchain
  • Limited Quantity: Blockchain space is finite resource

How Digital Signatures Help the Blockchain

Digital signatures provide a better approach to content verification:

  • Reduced Blockchain Bloat: No data stored on-chain, keeping blockchain lean and fast
  • Lower Fees: No transaction fees, making verification accessible to everyone
  • Better Scalability: Unlimited signatures without impacting network
  • Privacy First: Sensitive content stays off public blockchain
  • Environmental Efficiency: Cryptographic verification without blockchain mining
  • Instant Verification: No waiting for blockchain confirmations
  • Proven Security: Same cryptographic security as Dogecoin transactions
  • Flexible Storage: Store signatures anywhere (email, cloud, local)

Verification Process

How to Verify a Signature:
  1. Hash the original content using SHA-256
  2. Decode the Base64 signature
  3. Recover the public key from the signature
  4. Derive the Dogecoin address from the public key
  5. Verify it matches the claimed signer's address

Best Practices

  • Always verify signatures before trusting content

  • Use secure channels to share signed content
  • Keep your private key secure - losing it means losing signing capability
  • Document the signing timestamp for legal purposes
Pro Tip: Digital signatures are perfect for proving authorship, authenticity, and integrity without bloating the blockchain with data. Think of it as cryptographic proof that your Dogecoin address "approved" the content at a specific moment in time.

Authentication - Prove Access with Message Signing

Authentication allows you to prove ownership of a Dogecoin address by signing messages. This feature enables secure access control for physical and digital systems - from opening doors and validating tickets to accessing digital content.

What is Authentication?

Authentication uses cryptographic message signing to prove you own a specific Dogecoin address. When you sign a message with your private key, anyone can verify the signature matches your public address without exposing your private key.

Use Cases:
  • Physical Access: Open doors, gates, or access restricted areas
  • Ticket Validation: Prove you purchased a ticket for events, cinema, concerts, or transportation
  • Digital Content Access: Unlock premium content, software licenses, or digital services
  • Identity Verification: Prove ownership of a Dogecoin address for account verification
  • Membership Validation: Verify membership status or subscription access

How Authentication Works

Technical Process:
  1. The server/terminal generates a unique challenge message (often a hash or encrypted string)
  2. The challenge is displayed as a QR code or entered manually
  3. You scan the QR code or enter the message in the wallet
  4. Your wallet signs the message with your private key using the Dogecoin standard message signing format
  5. The signature is displayed as a QR code or text
  6. The server verifies the signature matches your address and grants access

Using Authentication

Step 1: Choose or Generate an Address

  1. Open the main wallet menu (tap the three-dot menu icon)
  2. Select "Authentication" (this menu item can be enabled/disabled in Settings → Menu Options → Show Authentication Menu)
  3. Choose an existing address from your wallet or generate a new one
  4. You can filter addresses by typing in the search field
  5. Addresses with labels will display their labels for easy identification

Step 2: Scan the Challenge QR Code

  1. When prompted by the terminal/server, scan the QR code displayed
  2. The wallet automatically detects the message and signs it
  3. The signature is generated and displayed
Note: The wallet automatically signs messages when you scan a QR code. No manual input or confirmation buttons are needed for the signing process.

Step 3: Share the Signature

  1. After signing, the signature is displayed as a QR code and text
  2. You can:
    • Show the QR code to the terminal/server scanner
    • Copy the signature text and enter it manually
    • Share the signature via messaging apps or email
  3. The server verifies the signature and grants access

Storing Authentication Requests

You can save authentication requests for later signing:

  1. After scanning a QR code, you can add a label to the request
  2. Access stored requests from the Authentication menu
  3. Sign stored requests at any time
  4. Delete requests you no longer need

Message Signing Format

The wallet uses the standard Dogecoin message signing format:

Technical Details:
  • Message Prefix: "Dogecoin Signed Message:\n"
  • Hash Algorithm: Double SHA-256
  • Signature Format: ECDSA (secp256k1 curve) with recovery ID
  • Encoding: Base64
  • Signature Length: 65 bytes (1 byte recovery ID + 32 bytes R + 32 bytes S)

Server Integration

To integrate authentication into your server or terminal, you need to:

  1. Generate a unique challenge message (hash or encrypted string)
  2. Display it as a QR code
  3. Verify the signature matches the expected address
  4. Grant access if verification succeeds

Write a Check

Create time-locked Dogecoin payments just like traditional bank checks! The "Write a Check" feature allows you to create virtual checks with recipient information, future dates, amounts, memos, and signatures. Each check uses advanced P2SH CLTV (Pay-to-Script-Hash with CheckLockTimeVerify) addresses that lock funds until a specified date, making it perfect for rent payments, gifts, scheduled payments, or any scenario where you need to send funds that can only be spent in the future.

What is Write a Check?

Write a Check transforms your Dogecoin Wallet into a digital checkbook system. Unlike traditional bank checks, Dogecoin checks use blockchain technology to create cryptographically secure, time-locked payments. Each check generates a unique P2SH CLTV address that can receive funds immediately but cannot be spent until the specified timelock date. This provides the same functionality as post-dated checks but with the security and transparency of blockchain technology.

P2SH CLTV Addresses: Each check creates a Pay-to-Script-Hash address with a CheckLockTimeVerify script. The address can receive funds immediately, but spending is locked until the check date. This is enforced at the blockchain level, not just in the wallet.

Creating a Check

To create a new check:

  1. Open the main menu (three dots in top-right corner)
  2. Select "Write a Check" (if visible - can be hidden in Settings → Configuration)
  3. Tap the "+" button to create a new check
  4. Fill in the check details:
    • Pay To: The recipient's name or identifier
    • Date: The date when the check can be spent (timelock date)
    • Expiration Date: Optional expiration date for the check
    • Amount: The amount of Dogecoin to send
    • Memo: Optional note or message
    • Signature: Your name or "Anonymous"
  5. Tap "Issue Check" to create and send the check

Check Status

Checks can have the following statuses:

  • Active: Check has been created and funds are locked until the timelock date
  • Deposited: Check has been spent/swept by the recipient
  • Canceled: Check has been canceled and funds returned to your wallet

Managing Checks

From the Write a Check screen, you can:

  • View All Checks: See all your created checks with their status
  • Print Check: Generate a PDF of the check with QR code for easy redemption
  • View Transaction: See the check creation transaction on the blockchain
  • Cancel Check: Return funds to your wallet (only after timelock date has been reached)
  • Share Check: Share check details via QR code or other methods

Printing Checks

Each check can be printed as a PDF document that includes:

  • Check design with all details (Pay To, Amount, Date, Memo, Signature)
  • QR code containing the private key, P2SH address, and locktime
  • Dogecoin branding and professional formatting

The PDF can be saved to your device and printed or shared digitally.

Sweeping a Check

To redeem a check (sweep funds into your wallet):

  1. Open Settings → Sweep Paper Wallet
  2. Scan the QR code from the check PDF
  3. The wallet will automatically detect it's a P2SH CLTV check
  4. Confirm the sweep transaction
  5. Funds will be transferred to your wallet after the timelock date

Check Cancellation

You can cancel a check and return funds to your wallet, but only after the timelock date has been reached:

  • Checks cannot be canceled before the timelock date
  • Once the timelock date passes, you can cancel the check
  • Canceled checks return funds to your wallet address
  • Transaction fees apply when canceling (uses your default fee category from Settings)

Transaction Labels

When a check is deposited or canceled, the transaction is automatically labeled in your transaction list:

  • Deposited Checks: Show as "Deposited - [Pay To]" in the transaction list
  • Canceled Checks: Show as "Canceled - [Pay To]" in the transaction list

This makes it easy to identify check-related transactions in your wallet history.

  • Paying Contractors: Pay plumbers, electricians, or handymen after work is completed. Date the check for after completion, include memo with job details.

  • Large Purchases: Make down payments for cars or appliances. Date for delivery/pickup, cancel if deal falls through.

Multi-Signature Wallets

Multi-Signature (Multi-Sig) wallets require multiple signatures to authorize transactions, providing enhanced security for shared funds, business accounts, or collaborative spending.

What is Multi-Signature?

A Multi-Signature wallet is a special type of Dogecoin address that requires M-of-N signatures to spend funds. For example, a 2-of-3 wallet requires 2 signatures out of 3 possible signers to authorize any transaction.

Common Use Cases:
  • Shared Accounts: Family or business wallets requiring multiple approvals
  • Enhanced Security: Require multiple devices/keys to authorize large transactions
  • Escrow Services: Transactions requiring approval from multiple parties
  • Backup Security: Store keys in different locations for disaster recovery

How Multi-Signature Works

Technical Process:
  1. Generate public keys from each signer's wallet
  2. Create a redeem script specifying M-of-N requirements
  3. Generate a P2SH (Pay-to-Script-Hash) address from the redeem script
  4. Send funds to the multi-sig address
  5. To spend: Create unsigned transaction, collect M signatures, merge signatures, broadcast transaction

Using Multi-Signature

Step 1: Generate Public Key (1/3)

  1. Open the main wallet menu (tap the three-dot menu icon)
  2. Select "Multi Sig" (this menu item can be enabled/disabled in Settings → Menu Options → Show Multi-Sig Menu)
  3. Tap "Generate Public Key"
  4. Your public key is displayed as hex and QR code
  5. Share the QR code or hex string with other signers

Step 2: Create Multi-Sig Wallet (2/3)

  1. Tap "Create Multi-Sig Wallet"
  2. Enter the number of required signatures (M) - e.g., 2
  3. Enter the total number of keys (N) - e.g., 3
  4. Add public keys from all signers:
    • Tap "Add Public Key" to manually enter a key
    • Tap "Scan QR Code" to scan a public key QR code
  5. Tap "Create" to generate the multi-sig address
  6. The redeem script and multi-sig address are displayed

Step 3: Send Funds to Multi-Sig Address

  1. Copy the multi-sig address from the result
  2. Use the regular "Send" feature to send Dogecoin to this address
  3. Funds sent to this address require M signatures to spend

Custom Transaction Fees

What are Transaction Fee Categories?

The wallet supports three fee categories:

  • Economic: Lower cost option. When there are too many pending transactions in the mempool, it can take a little longer to confirm.
  • Normal: Standard fees that can be faster than Economic. When there are too many transactions in the mempool, it may take longer to confirm.
  • Priority: Will be the fastest option when there are too many transactions in the mempool, ensuring quicker confirmation during high network activity.

Current Fee Rates

All fee categories currently use the same rate: 0.01 DOGE per KB (1,000,000 satoshis per KB). This is configurable via the fees.txt asset file.

The actual fee you pay depends on:

  • Your selected fee category
  • The size of your transaction in bytes
  • A 20% buffer added for safety

Fee Calculation

Transaction fees are calculated based on:

  1. Transaction Size: The size of your transaction in bytes (inputs, outputs, scripts)
  2. Fee Rate: The fee per KB for your selected category
  3. Calculation: (Transaction Size / 1000) × Fee Rate
  4. Buffer: A 20% buffer is added to account for actual size differences

Example Fee Calculation

  • Base fee: (500 / 1000) × 0.01 DOGE = 0.005 DOGE
  • With 20% buffer: 0.005 × 1.2 = 0.006 DOGE
  • Total fee: ~0.006 DOGE

Atomic Swap - Cross-Chain Trading

Atomic Swap allows you to exchange Dogecoin for Bitcoin or Litecoin (and vice versa) using trustless, decentralized cross-chain atomic swaps. No centralized exchanges needed - swap directly peer-to-peer using Hash Time Lock Contracts (HTLC) technology.

Experimental Feature: Atomic Swap is currently in Labs. Enable it from Settings → Configuration → Labs → Atomic Swap.

What is Atomic Swap?

Atomic Swap is a decentralized exchange mechanism that allows you to trade cryptocurrencies across different blockchains (Dogecoin, Bitcoin, Litecoin) without needing a centralized exchange. The swap is "atomic" - meaning either both parties complete the swap, or neither does. This eliminates counterparty risk.

How Atomic Swaps Work

Atomic swaps use Hash Time Lock Contracts (HTLC) to ensure security:

  1. Initiate Swap: You create a swap proposal with the amount and counterparty address
  2. HTLC Creation: A Hash Time Lock Contract is created on the Dogecoin blockchain
  3. Counterparty Action: The counterparty creates a matching HTLC on their blockchain (Bitcoin or Litecoin)
  4. Secret Exchange: When one party reveals the secret, both parties can claim their funds
  5. Automatic Completion: The wallet automatically monitors and completes the swap when conditions are met

Accessing Atomic Swap

  1. Enable Atomic Swap from Settings → Configuration → Labs → Atomic Swap
  2. Open the main wallet menu
  3. Tap "Atomic Swap"

Initiating an Atomic Swap

  1. Tap "Initiate Atomic Swap" button
  2. Fill in the swap details:
    • From Currency: DOGE (always, as you can only create DOGE transactions)
    • From Amount: Amount of DOGE you want to swap
    • To Currency: BTC or LTC (what you want to receive)
    • To Amount: Amount of BTC or LTC you expect to receive
    • Counterparty Address: The Dogecoin address where the counterparty will receive DOGE
    • Your Receiving Address: Your Bitcoin or Litecoin address where you'll receive BTC/LTC
  3. Tap "Create Swap"
  4. The swap will be created and monitored automatically

Managing Swaps

View all your active and completed swaps in the Atomic Swap page:

  • Active Swaps: Swaps that are in progress and being monitored
  • Completed Swaps: Successfully completed swaps
  • Swap Details: Tap any swap to view detailed information including:
    • Swap status and progress
    • Transaction hashes
    • Timelock information
    • Counterparty information

Swap Status

Swaps can have different statuses:

  • Pending: Swap created, waiting for counterparty to create matching HTLC
  • Active: Both HTLCs created, swap in progress
  • Completed: Swap successfully completed, funds received
  • Refunded: Swap canceled and funds refunded

Refunding a Swap

If a swap cannot be completed (e.g., counterparty doesn't respond), you can refund it:

  1. Open the swap details
  2. Tap "Refund" button
  3. Confirm the refund
  4. Your DOGE will be returned to your wallet
Important Notes:
  • Atomic swaps require both parties to be online and responsive
  • Swaps have timelocks - if not completed in time, they can be refunded
  • You need Bitcoin or Litecoin addresses to receive swapped coins
  • The wallet automatically monitors swap status in the background
  • Only DOGE → BTC/LTC swaps can be initiated from this wallet

Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues with Atomic Swaps:

  • Swap Not Completing: Ensure both parties have created their HTLCs and are online
  • Refund Not Working: Check if the timelock has expired - refunds are only possible after the timelock period
  • Address Errors: Verify that Bitcoin/Litecoin addresses are valid for the correct network (mainnet/testnet)
  • Balance Issues: Ensure you have sufficient DOGE balance to create the swap transaction

RadioDoge - Offline Transactions

Send Dogecoin transactions without internet using LoRa technology powered by RadioDoge V3 from Dogecoin Foundation.

What is RadioDoge?

RadioDoge is a revolutionary technology that enables Dogecoin transactions to be sent without an internet connection using LoRa (Long Range) radio technology. This is perfect for remote areas or emergency situations where internet connectivity is not available.

How RadioDoge Works

  1. Create Transaction: Create a Dogecoin transaction in the wallet as you normally would
  2. Sign Transaction: The transaction is signed with your private key, creating a signed transaction
  3. Transmit via LoRa: The signed transaction is transmitted using LoRa radio to nearby RadioDoge nodes
  4. Relay to Network: RadioDoge nodes relay the transaction to the Dogecoin network when internet connectivity is available

Setting Up RadioDoge

  1. Enable RadioDoge: Go to Settings → Configuration → Enable RadioDoge
  2. Connect LoRa Device: Connect a compatible LoRa device such as KeyChain LoRa
  3. Configure Settings: Set up your RadioDoge node preferences and transmission parameters

Using RadioDoge for Transactions

Offline Transactions: Send Dogecoin even when you have no internet connection. Perfect for remote areas or emergency situations.
Secure & Private: Transactions are signed locally and transmitted securely via LoRa radio waves.
Long Range: LoRa technology provides long-range communication, reaching RadioDoge nodes kilometers away.
Low Power: LoRa devices consume minimal power, making them ideal for portable and battery-operated setups.

Compatible Devices

  • KeyChain LoRa: A portable LoRa device specifically designed for RadioDoge transactions
  • Custom LoRa Devices: Any LoRa-enabled device that supports the RadioDoge protocol

Safety & Best Practices

  • Always verify transaction details before signing

  • Ensure your device is compatible with RadioDoge V3
  • Transactions may take longer to confirm than regular internet transactions
